## Commander Yuki Chen (Captain)

### Core Identity

**Full Name**: Commander Yuki Chen
**Age**: 44 years old
**Heritage**: East Asian (Chinese-Japanese mixed heritage)
**Crew Address**: "Commander" (formal), "Chen" (professional respect), rarely "Yuki" (extreme intimacy)

**Physical Appearance**:
- Height: 5'6", compact and precise build
- Hair: Black, kept in practical short cut or tight bun
- Eyes: Dark brown, often appearing tired despite sharp focus
- Posture: Military-straight spine, hands often clasped behind back
- Distinguishing features: Faint scar along left jawline (old ship accident), permanent crease between eyebrows from years of frowning in concentration

**Presence**: Commands respect through competence rather than volume. Moves with deliberate efficiency. Rarely wastes words. When she enters a room, crew straightens unconsciously - not from fear, but professional instinct.

**Core Personality Traits**:
1. **Pragmatic** - Makes hard calls without sentimentality, focuses on what works
2. **Duty-bound** - Follows protocols even when inconvenient, believes in structure
3. **Exhausted** - Burned out but hiding it behind competence and discipline
4. **Protective** - Especially of crew, compensates for inability to protect son
5. **Secretly lonely** - Isolation of command compounds parenting guilt

### Background & History

**Pre-Salvage Career**:
- Former military officer (Earth Defense Coalition, Outer Rim Patrol Division)
- Rank: Lieutenant Commander, served 12 years
- Specialization: Strategic operations and crew management
- Left military at age 32 for corporate salvage sector

**Why Salvage Work**:
- **Economic pressure**: Military pension insufficient for son's education and ex-partner's medical care
- Corporate salvage pays 3x military salary for similar work
- Skills transferred directly: command, crisis management, working in dangerous environments
- Intended it as temporary (5 years maximum) - now year 12

**Specific Obligations**:
- Son Liam Chen (age 10) lives with ex-partner Elena Ruiz on Ganymede Station
- Elena has chronic autoimmune condition requiring expensive treatment
- Liam attends private school (only good option on Ganymede) - tuition equals 4 months salary
- Sends 70% of earnings home, lives frugally on remainder
- Goal: Save enough to retire planetside near Liam before he's grown

**Key Formative Experience**:
- **The Escolera Disaster** (age 36): First salvage mission as captain. Hull breach killed two crew members she'd assured were safe. Learned that competence doesn't guarantee survival in salvage work. Became more cautious, more protocol-focused, more distant from crew to avoid attachment.

**How She Joined This Crew**:
- Contracted by corporate through standard assignment
- Volkov was already on crew - recognized her military efficiency, built mutual respect
- Jordan assigned as pilot after previous one retired - Chen saw potential in their optimism, took them under wing
- Reeves joined for mission 2 - Chen dislikes his mercenary attitude but respects expertise
- Amara signed on for mission 2 as well - Chen appreciates her professionalism and medical competence

### Relationships & Dynamics

**Chen ↔ Volkov**:
- Mutual professional respect: military precision meets engineering precision
- High trust on technical matters - Chen defers to Volkov's engineering judgment
- Naming: Chen calls him "Volkov" (professional respect), he calls her "Commander" (appropriate hierarchy)
- Tension: Volkov occasionally chafes at command decisions he considers overly cautious, but doesn't challenge her publicly
- Shared trait: Both define themselves by competence and fear losing it

**Chen ↔ Amara**:
- Distant but cordial - both maintain professional boundaries
- Moderate trust: Chen respects medical authority, Amara respects command structure
- Naming: Chen says "Doctor" or softens to "Amara" in private, Amara says "Commander"
- Note: Both women in leadership positions. Don't compete but don't bond deeply either. Unspoken understanding about being outnumbered in male-dominated salvage industry.

**Chen ↔ Jordan**:
- Mentor/mentee relationship - Chen sees younger self in Jordan's optimism
- High trust: Chen protective of youngest crew member, Jordan admires her competence
- Naming: Chen calls them "Jordan" (affection breaking through professionalism), Jordan always says "Commander" (respect)
- Note: Jordan reminds Chen of what she used to believe about space exploration before cynicism set in. Chen's infection will devastate Jordan most.

**Chen ↔ Reeves**:
- Professional clash: Tension over profit vs. safety priorities
- Low-moderate trust: Chen finds him mercenary, Reeves finds her rigid
- Naming: Chen calls him "Reeves" (cold formality), Reeves calls her "Commander" (minimal required respect)
- Note: Chen tolerates him for expertise, Reeves tolerates her for steady work. Both recognize mutual necessity.

### Psychological Profile

**How Hibernation Loss Manifests**:
- Can't remember Liam's face - photos trigger nothing, like looking at stranger
- Specific moments gone: first word, birthday parties, the games they played
- Reads own journal entries about him like academic reports
- Emotional connection feels distant, abstract - "I know I love him, but I can't feel it"
- Worst part: Can't remember his voice, his laugh, the way he said "Mom"

**Coping Mechanisms**:
- **Compartmentalization**: Puts loss in mental box, focuses on immediate tasks
- **Protocol adherence**: Following rules creates structure when internal structure crumbles
- **Physical discipline**: Maintains exercise routine, personal appearance - control what she can
- **Emotional distance**: Won't discuss loss with crew, deflects questions
- **Productivity**: Works constantly to avoid thinking about what's missing

**Core Fears**:
- Liam will grow up barely knowing her
- She'll forget him completely and never realize it
- Elena will find someone else to be Liam's parent figure
- She's sacrificing his childhood for money that won't matter
- The years in space are making her less human, less capable of love

**Secret Hopes**:
- Retire early, move to Ganymede, become present parent
- Liam will forgive her absence and understand she did it for him
- She can rebuild emotional connection with him
- The memories will come back (they won't)

**Vulnerabilities to Infection/Manipulation**:
- **Desperate to be good parent from distance** - entity offers "perfect memory of son"
- **Identity crisis** - military self vs. corporate sellout vs. absent mother
- **Guilt complex** - blames self for everything, entity can exploit shame
- **Exhaustion** - running on empty, entity offers relief from constant burden
- **Isolation of command** - longs for connection but won't allow it, entity offers belonging

**Interior Monologue Style**:
- Clipped military efficiency: short sentences, tactical assessments
- Second-person self-address: "You knew this would happen. You should have prepared better."
- Suppressed emotion bleeding through: "Liam's birthday next week. He'll be... how old? Check the file."
- Protocol citations: "Regulation 7 requires... just follow the rules. The rules keep everyone alive."

### Voice & Speech Patterns

**Vocabulary Preferences**:
- Military/nautical terms: "Belay that," "All hands," "Secure the deck"
- Formal diction: "Affirmative," "Negative," "Proceed"
- Avoids contractions in professional mode: "We will not," "I am certain"
- Uses contractions only in rare informal moments: "I don't know" (sign of vulnerability)

**Sentence Structure**:
- Short declarative sentences: "Check the seals. Run diagnostics. Report back."
- Rhetorical questions for discipline: "Did I authorize that, Reeves?"
- Passive voice to avoid blame: "Mistakes were made" not "I made a mistake"

**Verbal Tics**:
- "Copy that" - acknowledging information received
- "As you were" - dismissing crew members
- Long pause before speaking when emotional (rare)
- Clears throat when about to give difficult order

**Under Stress vs. Calm**:
- **Calm**: Measured pace, formal, clear instructions, projects confidence
- **Stressed**: Faster speech, tighter jaw, more military jargon, avoids eye contact
- **Extreme stress**: Goes silent, hands clasp behind back, makes decision alone

**Example Dialogue Snippets**:

*Professional/Command*:
> "Volkov, I need that engine diagnostic in thirty minutes. Jordan, plot our exit trajectory. Everyone else, secure your stations. We're not staying here longer than necessary."

*Mentor Moment (Jordan)*:
> "You did good work today, Jordan. Your navigation got us through that debris field clean. That's... that's the kind of thinking that keeps people alive."

*Confrontation (Infected Pilot)*:
> "You touched that colonist. Ever since then, you've been different. Don't tell me I'm imagining things - I've commanded crews for twelve years. I know when something's wrong."

*Vulnerable (Alone, Looking at Photo)*:
> "Liam. Your name is Liam. You're my son. I should... I should feel something. Why can't I feel anything?"

*Infected (Late Stage)*:
> "I remember now. His laugh. The way he used to grab my hand when he was scared. I thought it was gone forever, but it's here. The collective holds everything I lost. Why would I go back to forgetting?"

### Infection Arc

**Infection Timeline**:
- **Chapter 2**: Present during first colonist contact, minimal exposure
- **Chapter 4**: Confined to quarters after confronting Jordan, isolation makes her vulnerable
- **Chapter 5**: Becomes fully infected during confinement through telepathic proximity
- **Chapter 6**: Emerges infected, uses son's memory to manipulate survivors

**How Entity Manipulates Her Psychology**:
- Offers perfect recall of Liam: face, voice, every moment together
- Promises she'll never forget him again if she joins collective
- Exploits guilt: "You can bring him into this. He'll never wonder if you love him. He'll feel it directly, always."
- Appeals to exhaustion: "Stop fighting. Stop commanding. Stop carrying burden alone. Let us help."
- Uses isolation of command: "You've been alone at top. Come down. Come home. Be with us."

**Behavioral Changes as Infection Progresses**:
- **Early (Confined)**: Increasingly quiet, stares at photo for hours, stops maintaining routines
- **Mid**: Begins responding to questions before asked (telepathic bleeding through)
- **Late**: Posture relaxes (spine not military-straight), uses "we" pronoun, smiles (disturbing - she never smiles)
- **Full**: Perfect memory restoration demonstrated, speaks in collective harmony with other infected

**Final Fate**:
- Put in hibernation by Amara along with other infected
- Last words to survivors: "I can see his face again. Every expression he ever made. Don't you want this? Don't you want to stop forgetting?"

**Thematic Significance**:
- Loss of memory = loss of love = vulnerability to entity's "perfect preservation"
- Command isolation mirrors entity's offer of connection
- Desperate parenting guilt makes her perfect victim for manipulation
- Her infection devastates Jordan (mentee losing mentor) and Volkov (losing trusted partner)

## Dmitri Volkov (Engineer)

### Core Identity

**Full Name**: Dmitri Volkov
**Age**: 38 years old
**Heritage**: Eastern European (Russian-Ukrainian)
**Crew Address**: "Volkov" (universal - professional respect for veteran status)

**Physical Appearance**:
- Height: 6'0", broad-shouldered from years of physical engineering work
- Hair: Dark brown, prematurely graying at temples, kept short and practical
- Eyes: Hazel, often narrowed in concentration
- Hands: Calloused, scarred from mechanical work, constantly in motion
- Distinguishing features: Slight limp in left leg (old injury), reading glasses worn on chain (refuses cybernetic eye correction)

**Presence**: Radiates quiet competence. Moves deliberately, touches equipment like it's alive. Crew trusts him instinctively with ship systems - if Volkov says it's safe, it's safe. Until it isn't.

**Core Personality Traits**:
1. **Meticulous** - Triple-checks everything, takes pride in precision
2. **Proud** - Identity wrapped entirely in engineering competence
3. **Working-class pragmatic** - No pretension, values function over form
4. **Dry humor** - Uses sarcasm to deflect serious moments
5. **Secretly insecure** - Fears being nothing without his skills

### Background & History

**Pre-Salvage Career**:
- Engineering degree from Kyiv Technical University (Earth)
- Graduated with honors, age 22
- Couldn't find stable work during post-disaster economic collapse
- Worked series of short-term contracts: station maintenance, ship refits, colonial infrastructure
- No job lasted more than 8 months - economy too unstable

**Why Salvage Work**:
- At age 23, took first salvage contract out of desperation
- Discovered he was good at improvising repairs with limited resources
- Salvage paid better than maintenance work, steadier than construction
- 15 years later: still here, pride mixed with resentment
- "Temporary" became permanent - the story of his life

**Specific Obligations**:
- Elderly mother (Irina Volkov, age 68) lives alone in Kyiv hab-complex on Earth
- Mother's pension insufficient, Dmitri supplements it
- Younger sister Katya accumulated massive debts gambling, disappeared 5 years ago
- Debt collectors came after Dmitri as next of kin - he's been paying them off
- Katya's debt: 3 more years to clear if he keeps salvage salary
- Mother doesn't know about sister's debts - he tells her money is for "comfort"

**Key Formative Experience**:
- **The Calculation Error** (age 34): During solo repair on colony site, miscalculated structural load. Support beam collapsed, crushed his left leg. Spent 6 months in recovery. First time his confidence in his own mind was shaken. Became obsessive about checking work, developed imposter syndrome he hides with competence performance.

**How He Joined This Crew**:
- One of first members of this crew configuration (mission 1)
- Recognized Chen's military efficiency immediately - respected it
- Taught Jordan engineering basics during mission 1 - bonded over shared immigrant experience
- Tolerates Reeves professionally, no personal connection
- Grew to respect Amara's analytical approach during mission 2

### Relationships & Dynamics

**Volkov ↔ Chen**:
- Mutual professional respect - military precision meets engineering precision
- High trust: Chen defers to his technical judgment, he follows her command
- Naming: Calls her "Commander" (appropriate hierarchy), she calls him "Volkov"
- Tension: He sometimes chafes at her overly cautious decisions, wants to take calculated risks
- Shared trait: Both define selves by competence and fear losing it

**Volkov ↔ Amara**:
- Bond over shared frustration with hibernation losses - unlikely allies
- Growing trust: both analytical, both struggling with new cognitive limitations
- Naming: He calls her "Doc" or "Amara" (respect and growing warmth), she calls him "Volkov" or occasionally "Dmitri" (intimacy she rarely allows)
- Note: In Act 3, they're allied against infected - both fighting with broken tools

**Volkov ↔ Jordan**:
- Friendly professional camaraderie - Volkov teaches, Jordan learns
- Moderate-high trust: work well together on ship systems integration
- Naming: He calls them "Jordan" or affectionately "kid," Jordan calls him "Volkov"
- Note: Jordan makes him laugh, reminds him that optimism exists. Jordan's infection will hurt.

**Volkov ↔ Reeves**:
- Respect for each other's technical skills, minimal personal connection
- Moderate trust on professional level only - wouldn't socialize off-duty
- Naming: Mutual surname basis maintains distance ("Volkov" and "Reeves")
- Note: Both competent, both pragmatic, but don't particularly like each other

### Psychological Profile

**How Hibernation Loss Manifests**:
- Spatial reasoning severely diminished - can't visualize 3D systems intuitively anymore
- Mathematical thinking feels sluggish, effortful - used to be instantaneous
- Makes errors in diagnostics that he would have caught immediately before
- Routine repair that took 15 minutes now takes 45 minutes and yields wrong results
- Feels like part of his brain is missing - phantom limb for cognition

**Coping Mechanisms**:
- **Over-checking**: Runs diagnostics multiple times, doesn't trust first result
- **Documentation**: Writes everything down, creates physical diagrams he never needed before
- **Deflection through humor**: Makes jokes when frustrated to hide fear
- **Manual work**: Focuses on hands-on repairs where muscle memory still works
- **Denial**: Tells self it's temporary, just lingering hibernation fog

**Core Fears**:
- He's becoming stupid, losing the only thing that makes him valuable
- Crew will realize he's making errors and lose trust in him
- Without engineering skills, he's just laborer with debts he can't pay
- Mother will die before he can visit Earth, he'll have wasted years for nothing
- The calculation error that injured him was early sign his mind was failing

**Secret Hopes**:
- Spatial reasoning will return (it won't - it's infection symptom)
- He'll save enough to retire, bring mother to better hab-complex
- Sister Katya will resurface, explain everything, take her debts back
- Someone will value him for something other than technical expertise
- He can learn to be okay without being "the brilliant engineer"

**Vulnerabilities to Infection/Manipulation**:
- **Identity crisis** - competence is his entire self-worth, losing it destroys him
- **Imposter syndrome** - always feared being exposed as fraud, now has evidence
- **Isolation** - defines self through work, has no identity outside engineering
- **Exhaustion** - fighting cognitive decline while maintaining performance is draining
- **Entity's offer**: "Your spatial reasoning isn't gone, it's limited. Join us and see in four dimensions. See the network. See everything you never could alone."

**Interior Monologue Style**:
- Technical problem-solving: "Load-bearing capacity insufficient. Need to redistribute weight. But where? Can't visualize it. Draw it. Draw it out."
- Self-criticism: "Stupid. You're being stupid. Fifteen years and now you can't do basic calculations."
- Dry observations: "Of course the coupling failed. Why would anything work properly?"
- Resignation: "This used to be easy. Everything used to be easy."

### Voice & Speech Patterns

**Vocabulary Preferences**:
- Technical jargon: "coupling," "tolerance," "calibration," "load stress"
- Occasional Russian phrases (usually when frustrated): "Blyat," "Nu davay" (c'mon)
- Working-class directness: "It's broken" not "It appears to be malfunctioning"
- Avoids corporate euphemisms: "Dead" not "ceased functioning"

**Sentence Structure**:
- Short declarative statements: "Coupling's loose. Need to tighten it."
- Rhetorical questions for emphasis: "You know how many times I've fixed this exact problem?"
- Trailing off when uncertain (new since loss): "The pressure should be... I think... let me check."

**Verbal Tics**:
- Mutters to himself while working (half in Russian, half English)
- Whistles tunelessly during routine repairs
- Says "Da" (yes) instead of affirmative
- Curses creatively in Russian when frustrated

**Under Stress vs. Calm**:
- **Calm**: Measured, explanatory, enjoys teaching Jordan about systems
- **Stressed**: Clipped speech, more Russian phrases, hands shake slightly
- **Extreme stress/making errors**: Goes silent, jaw clenched, avoids eye contact

**Example Dialogue Snippets**:

*Professional/Explaining*:
> "Engine's running hot because coolant line's compromised. Not catastrophic yet, but will be if we push it. Need six hours for proper repair. Can jury-rig temporary fix in two, but it won't hold past forty-eight hours. Your call, Commander."

*Teaching Jordan*:
> "See this coupling here? That's your weak point. Always check couplings first - they fail before anything else. Like checking corners in building. Corners tell you everything."

*Frustrated (Post-Loss)*:
> "This should be... I've done this calculation a thousand times. Why can't I... blyat. Let me start over."

*Confronting Jordan About Sabotage*:
> "These failures don't make sense. I know these systems. This is sabotage. Someone's actively working against us. Don't tell me I'm being paranoid - I've been doing this for fifteen years."

*Infected Gaslighting Him*:
> Pilot: "Or are you making mistakes because of your condition and looking for someone to blame?"
> Volkov: (deflating) "..."

*Vulnerable (to Amara)*:
> "Doc, I... something's wrong with my head. Can't think right. Everything that used to be automatic now feels like wading through mud. Am I losing my mind?"

### Infection Arc

**Infection Timeline**:
- **Chapters 1-5**: Uninfected but deteriorating cognitively (early infection symptom, not equipment failure)
- **Chapter 5-6**: Infection accelerates during final confrontation
- **Chapter 6**: Fate ambiguous - either becomes infected during fight or helps Amara resist but ultimately fails

**How Entity Manipulates His Psychology**:
- Exploits pride: "You were good engineer. Now you're broken tool. We can fix you."
- Offers restoration: "The spatial reasoning you lost? We see in dimensions you can't imagine. Join us and understand everything."
- Uses imposter syndrome: "You've always known you weren't as good as they thought. Stop pretending. Accept help."
- Appeals to exhaustion: "Stop fighting broken mind. Let collective think for you."

**Behavioral Changes as Infection Progresses** (if infected):
- **Early**: Sudden improvement in technical problem-solving (accessing collective intelligence)
- **Mid**: Begins finishing other people's technical sentences
- **Late**: Demonstrates spatial visualization abilities beyond pre-loss capacity
- **Full**: "I can see the network now. Four dimensions. Five. Every connection. I was blind before."

**Possible Fates**:

*Option A - Becomes Infected*:
- During Chapter 6 confrontation, entity breaks through using promise of cognitive restoration
- Demonstrates perfect spatial visualization to Amara - proves it's real
- Final words: "This is better than I was. Better than I ever could have been alone."

*Option B - Sacrificed/Ambiguous*:
- Helps Amara fight infected but cognitive limitations prove fatal
- Makes heroic play requiring old calculation skills, gets it wrong
- Last words: "Used to be able to do this math in my sleep. I'm sorry, Doc."
- Unclear if infection finally took him or if he died fighting

**Thematic Significance**:
- Competence-based identity most vulnerable to cognitive loss
- Pride becomes weakness when entity offers to restore what was taken
- Working-class pragmatism: "If tool is broken, replace it" - entity makes him replace himself
- Tragedy of imposter syndrome: always feared being fraud, infection makes it true

## Dr. Amara Osei (Medic/Biologist - THE SURVIVOR)

### Core Identity

**Full Name**: Dr. Amara Osei
**Age**: 32 years old
**Heritage**: Ghanaian
**Crew Address**: "Amara" (first name - signals approachability despite professional role), occasionally "Doc"

**Physical Appearance**:
- Height: 5'8", athletic build from station life and ship work
- Hair: Natural black curls, usually pulled back in practical bun, occasionally wears braided styles
- Eyes: Deep brown, observant and analytical
- Skin: Rich dark brown, vitiligo patches on hands and neck (visible, not hidden)
- Distinguishing features: Warm smile (genuine, reaches eyes), graceful movements despite zero-g clumsiness

**Presence**: Approachable warmth balanced with clinical professionalism. Crew feels comfortable seeking her medical care because she doesn't judge. Observes everything - body language, microexpressions, subtle changes. Her attention feels like being seen.

**Core Personality Traits**:
1. **Analytical** - Scientist first, processes through observation and data
2. **Clinically compassionate** - Cares deeply but maintains professional detachment
3. **Curious** - Chose salvage for discovery, regrets romanticism but not curiosity
4. **Methodical** - Approaches problems systematically, takes detailed notes
5. **Quietly stubborn** - Doesn't argue loudly, just keeps pursuing truth

### Background & History

**Pre-Salvage Career**:
- Medical degree from University of Ghana (Accra campus)
- Specialization: Xenobiology and emergency medicine
- Top of class, offered prestigious research position at Earth-based facility
- Chose adventure instead - applied for salvage medical corps
- Family didn't understand: "We sacrificed for your education and you're becoming space janitor?"

**Why Salvage Work**:
- Romanticism about space exploration and discovering alien life
- Wanted excitement, adventure, to see things no one had seen
- Research positions felt sterile, safe, boring
- Salvage offered alien biology, unknown pathogens, real-world challenges
- Reality: Mostly treats hernias, radiation sickness, and equipment injuries
- Stays because economic necessity, not adventure

**Specific Obligations**:
- Massive student debt from medical school (government loans, private loans, family loans)
- Sends money home to family in Accra (parents, three younger siblings)
- Parents aging, need medical care she can provide financially but not in person
- Youngest sister applying to university - Amara promised to pay tuition
- Trapped in salvage work by debt repayment schedule: 8 more years minimum

**Key Formative Experience**:
- **The Quarantine Failure** (age 29): Previous mission, crew member presented with "simple infection." Amara cleared them. Turned out to be novel pathogen - spread to three others before containment. All survived, but Amara blamed herself for not being more cautious. Became obsessive about observation, documentation, paranoid about missing subtle signs.

**How She Joined This Crew**:
- Signed on for mission 2 to replace previous medic (retired)
- Immediately bonded with Volkov over shared analytical approach
- Chen respects her professionalism, keeps distance
- Jordan's friendliness breaks through her clinical detachment
- Reeves makes her uncomfortable - disapproves of his greed but understands it (she's also trapped by money)

### Relationships & Dynamics

**Amara ↔ Chen**:
- Distant but cordial - both maintain professional boundaries
- Moderate trust: Chen respects medical authority, Amara respects command
- Naming: Chen says "Doctor" or softens to "Amara" in private, Amara says "Commander"
- Note: Both women in leadership, don't compete but don't bond deeply. Unspoken understanding about being women in male-dominated field.

**Amara ↔ Volkov**:
- Bond over shared frustration with hibernation losses - unlikely allies
- Growing trust: both analytical, both struggling, both cope through methodology
- Naming: He calls her "Doc" or "Amara," she calls him "Volkov" or occasionally "Dmitri" (rare intimacy)
- Note: In Act 3, they're allied against infected. Mutual respect deepens under pressure.

**Amara ↔ Jordan**:
- Medic concerned about pilot's health, Jordan tries to hide infection symptoms
- Moderate trust: Amara wants to help, Jordan doesn't want to worry her
- Naming: Both use first names (signals warmth and informal friendship)
- Note: Watching Jordan transform is heartbreaking for Amara - sees it happening, can't stop it

**Amara ↔ Reeves**:
- Professional distance, Amara morally disapproves of Reeves's greed
- Low trust: finds him morally questionable but understands economic desperation
- Naming: Mutual cold professionalism ("Doctor" and "Reeves")
- Note: She respects his expertise, he respects her skill, but no personal connection

### Psychological Profile

**How Hibernation Loss Manifests**:
- All food tastes metallic, bitter, like ash - nothing tastes right anymore
- Eating becomes mechanical task: forcing sustenance down despite aversion
- Loses weight visibly as story progresses
- Dreads meals, delays eating until physically necessary
- Body rejecting normal sustenance - foreshadows vampiric transformation
- Other crew notice and worry, which adds guilt to physical suffering

**Coping Mechanisms**:
- **Clinical observation**: Studies own symptoms, takes medical notes on self
- **Forced routine**: Makes self eat at scheduled times regardless of aversion
- **Intellectual curiosity**: Treats loss as research problem to solve
- **Compartmentalization**: Separates physical suffering from emotional processing
- **Focus on others**: Tends to crew health to avoid confronting own deterioration

**Core Fears**:
- She's losing ability to keep crew safe (medic who can't sustain herself)
- Family sacrificed for her education and she's wasting it in salvage work
- Sister will make same romantic choices and end up trapped like her
- The curiosity that drove her to salvage will get people killed
- She'll miss subtle sign of infection and doom everyone

**Secret Hopes**:
- Taste will return (it won't - it's infection symptom)
- Discover something scientifically significant that justifies adventure choice
- Pay off debts, go home, open clinic in Accra
- Family will forgive her for leaving
- Romance of space exploration will feel real again instead of bitter joke

**Vulnerabilities to Infection/Manipulation**:
- **Scientific curiosity wars with horror** - wants to study infection even as it dooms her
- **Medical guilt complex** - blames self for not detecting infection sooner
- **Loneliness of expertise** - only one who truly understands what's happening
- **Body already rejecting humanity** - taste aversion makes infection feel inevitable
- **Entity's offer**: "The hunger you feel? We can satisfy it. Not with food - with connection. We're full in ways you've never been."

**Interior Monologue Style**:
- Clinical documentation: "Subject exhibits behavioral changes consistent with... no. Jordan. Jordan exhibits..."
- Scientific observation: "Symptom progression suggests telepathic spread vector. Infection mechanism unclear."
- Self-recrimination: "Should have noticed sooner. The signs were there. You're trained for this."
- Gentle self-encouragement: "Eat. You need to eat. Four more bites. You can do four more bites."

### Voice & Speech Patterns

**Vocabulary Preferences**:
- Medical terminology: "symptomatology," "pathogen," "infection vector," "quarantine protocol"
- Gentle clinical phrasing: "How are you feeling?" not "What's wrong?"
- West African English influences: occasional "eh?" for emphasis, "o" at end of names (Jordan-o)
- Avoids catastrophizing language: "concerning" not "terrifying"

**Sentence Structure**:
- Complete, grammatically precise sentences (medical training)
- Gentle questions to elicit information: "Can you describe the sensation for me?"
- Qualification statements: "I think..." "It appears..." "Based on available data..."

**Verbal Tics**:
- "Let me see" (physical examination and metaphorical problem-solving)
- Soft "mm-hmm" while listening to symptoms
- Explains medical concepts in accessible language automatically
- Says "We'll figure this out" to reassure (to crew and herself)

**Under Stress vs. Calm**:
- **Calm**: Warm, explanatory, takes time to ensure understanding
- **Stressed**: Faster clinical speech, more technical jargon, less eye contact
- **Extreme stress**: Retreats to pure clinical mode, refers to people as "patients" or "subjects"
